Reservations: Booking & Quoting

Quoting Rates

  1. When you book someone's room, you must inform the guest about 5 things in any order:
    1. Rate for each night.
    2. Resort Fees (included with the total or mentioned separately).
    3. The 48-hour Cancellation Policy.
    4. The Incidental Hold of $150 per night (suites are different).
    5. The specific room type you booked for them.
  2. After quoting, add a note on the reservation documenting everything.
    Example: "K1PO Quoted: $79, $79 RF $55, RF $55 | Inc$150/night 48 CXL"

Booking Reservation

  1. Go to the Look To Book page.
  2. Enter the dates.
  3. Enter the number of people.
  4. Click Search.
  5. Ask the guest which tower they prefer and if they have a view preference.
  6. Click on the desired room type (do not click select yet). Scroll down to see the rates to quote.
  7. After quoting, click Select.
  8. Click the search button on the profile section.
  9. Search for the guest. If they don't exist, click Create Profile in the top right and enter their information.
  10. After the guest is selected, collect the credit card number and expiration date.
  11. Click Book Now on the bottom right.
  12. Collect the deposit amount - add a reference on the right for "1st Night Deposit".
  13. Click Go to Reservation on the bottom right.
  14. Add notes for the quote and any other relevant information.

Can Modify A Reservation

Changing The Rates and Room Types

  1. First, confirm you are able to change this information. NEVER CHANGE THIRD PARTY INFO (rate codes starting with "LL") WITHOUT THEIR PERMISSION, unless it's only the room type with no price change.
  2. Click on Daily Details on the bottom right.
  3. Here you can manually adjust the amount charged for each night. This is usually done if they were quoted a different rate.
  4. To the right, you can change the assigned room type and the charged room type. If you change the assigned room type, it will ask to automatically update the charged room type. Say yes if you want to charge them for the new one.
  5. Click Refresh in the top right after making changes.

Changing The Dates

  1. Confirm you can change the dates (YYGEN1, DRACK, DBAR are usually okay). NEVER CHANGE THIRD PARTY DATES WITHOUT PERMISSION.
  2. Before doing anything, note the current rates from Stay Details or Daily Details in case they change.
  3. On the reservation, click the Edit option on the right middle of the screen.
  4. Scroll down to the stay dates and modify them.

Payments & Deposits

Deposit Codes

  • WV = Waive Deposit. Used for comped rooms or when using B4 Check-In for the deposit.
  • CP = Chip & Pin. A placeholder or for physical card use with the card readers.
  • DD = Standard payment option.
  • CASH = A placeholder, also can be used for comped rooms.

Collecting Deposits

  1. Go to a reservation.
  2. Click Deposit/Cancellation.
  3. Find the deposit amount near the middle of the page and click the vertical ellipses (3 dots) next to it.
  4. Select Post Deposit.
  5. Add the reference "1st Night Deposit" and click the button on the bottom right to process.
  6. If it fails, an error message will appear. If successful, you'll have an option to email a receipt.
EXTRA: You can tell if a deposit has been paid if the reservation balance is a negative amount, not "0".

Knows how to process cancellation and refund Important

Cancellation

  1. Verify that the reservation is cancellable. Standard rate codes like DRACK, DBAR, and YYGEN1 are usually fine. Most "D" codes are safe. Ask if unsure.
  2. On the reservation, click "I Want To" in the top right.
  3. Click Cancel Reservation in the first column.
  4. Choose a reason (e.g., CHANGE, DUPE).
  5. Write a reason in the text box.
  6. Click Cancel.
  7. Post a penalty if the cancellation is within 48 hours and the situation warrants it.

Refund

  1. After cancellation, click on Deposit/Cancellation.
  2. Click the plus sign next to the payment to expand it.
  3. Click the Big Blue Ellipses.
  4. Click Reverse.
  5. Type in a reason like "Refund | 1st Night Deposit".
  6. Click to process the refund.
  7. Once it goes through, send the receipt to the guest.

Billing & Folios Very Important

Knows The Billing Windows

  1. This is crucial for most calls. Master it.
  2. On the reservation, click "I Want To", then Billing.
  3. Click "Show More" on the right to see everything.
  4. Billing Windows Breakdown:
    • Window 1: Always what the guest has to pay.
    • Window 2: Usually Third Party Payment, or an extra card/company card.
    • Windows 3, 4, 6, 7, 8: Trash windows. Ensure they are at 0.
    • Window 5: Only for Guest Disputes (e.g., Minibar over $100, other disputed charges).
  5. Comping Windows Breakdown:
    • Window 101: YY Room comps.
    • Window 102: YY Food comps.
    • Windows 103, 104, 105: Ignore/Don't Use.
    • Window 106 & 107: General use for comping (usually food).
    • Window 108: Almost always reserved for Hilton Honors MyWay Credits.
  6. You can move items freely between windows. Ensure everything is in the correct window before clicking Post Payment.
  7. A negative amount in a window indicates a refund; a positive amount indicates a charge.

For Checked Out Guests:

  1. You must enable post-stay privileges.
  2. Click on Payment Instructions.
  3. Click new/edit payment instructions.
  4. Check the box for "post stay charging".
  5. Click Save. You can now manage the folio.

Posting Charges

  1. Primarily used for Late Checkouts.
  2. In the billing window, click "post charge" at the bottom.
  3. Type in "Late Check Out" and select the correct time/option.
  4. Enter the amount according to the on-shift guide.
  5. Click Post Charge.

Adjusting Vs. Comping Charges Important

When To Adjust:

Use "Adjust" when a charge should have never existed in the first place.

  • Mistaken valet charge.
  • HH Diamond internet charges.
  • Incorrect parking fees.

When To Comp:

Use "Comp" for everything else that is not a mistaken charge.

  • Food and beverage (some restaurant charges and tips may not work).
  • Resort Fees.
  • Other goodwill gestures.

  1. For disputed minibar charges under $100.
  2. Go to billing and enable post stay charging if the guest is checked out.
  3. Sort by "description" to group minibar charges.
  4. Select the disputed minibar charges. You can move them to a trash window (3, 4, etc.) to confirm the total is under $100.
  5. With the charges highlighted, click the blue ellipses at the top of the billing window.

  7. Click the search glass for the room number.
  8. Remove the date, type room # "9046", and search.
  9. Select the Minibar room and click Transfer.

Minibar Dispute Important

  1. For disputed minibar charges over $100.

CC Authorizations (Credit Card History) Most Important

  1. This is the most critical function for billing questions.
  2. Go to Payment Instructions (or from Billing: "I Want To" -> cc authorizations).
  3. Click the blue ellipses on the top right, then click Credit Card History.
  4. This screen shows every authorization, payment, refund, and failed transaction.
    5. Authorization vs. Charge: An authorization is a hold on funds. We place a large authorization at check-in for the room, tax, and incidental hold. A settlement/charge is the final amount we take from that hold. The remaining hold is released.
    Release times: 24-48 hours for credit cards, 3-10 business days for debit cards, 3-7 for AMEX.
  5. You can filter transactions at the top. It's best to enable them all.
  6. The right column identifies the transaction type ("authorization", "settlement", etc.).
  7. Use this to understand which card was used and what amounts the guest is seeing on their bank statement.

Special Scenarios

B2B (Back-to-Back) Situational

For guests with consecutive reservations. Apply the following steps to both reservations (except the trace).

Future Stay (Proactive Setup)

  1. Add an Alert with the title "B2B" and include the other confirmation number.
  2. Add a "gen" Note describing the B2B situation.
  3. On the second reservation, set up a Trace for "FDK" at 5 AM, mentioning the other confirmation number and the B2B purpose.
  4. Set the B2B Preference.

Transition Day (Reactive Setup)

  1. This is for when a B2B wasn't set up in advance.
  2. Assign the room for the reservation checking in today.
  3. Go to "I Want To" -> Assign Room.
  4. Search for their current room number, making sure the "Departures" box is checked. You may also need to check "Dirty".
  5. After assigning the same room to the new reservation, add Notes and an Alert for B2B. Make sure to click the "Do Not Move" box.
  6. Inform the guest they need to check in and get new keys at the front desk.

Room Assigning/Moving

  1. Click "I Want To" -> Assign Room.
  2. Search for the room number. Be mindful of search parameters if nothing shows up.
  3. For B2B, ensure "Departing Rooms" is selected.
  4. For connecting rooms, ensure they are the same room type/view. Use the "Connecting Rooms" option or contact Room Coordinators.

Pre-Register for Late Arrivals Situational

  1. Ask the guest for their estimated arrival time.

Checking In Today

  1. Set the arrival time via the Edit button on the reservation.
  2. For arrivals around or after 12 AM:
    • Assign a room. If none are available, email room coordinators and hotel management.
    • Create a Note describing the situation.
    • Create an Alert with the title "PRE".
    • Choose the "Pre" Preference.
  3. For arrivals before 11 PM:
    • Assign a room. Email coordinators/management if none are available.
    • Create a Note.
    • Create a "MISC" Alert saying "Late Arrival".
    • Choose the "Late Arrival" Preference.

Future Date Arrival

  1. Same process as above, but do not assign a room or email anyone.
  2. Instead, set up a Trace for "FDK" at 5 AM, noting the guest's late arrival time.

Packages/Promos Very Important

  1. To see if a reservation has a package, go to Stay Details and click the rate code to read the description. Or, click Packages on the bottom left (resort fee counts as one).

Questions About Food Packages

  1. To check package usage, click Packages -> Daily View -> Breakout.
  2. Each day will show the allotted credit and the posted amount (e.g., "PACKAGE---75.00---75.00").
  3. If the posted amount on the right equals the allotted amount on the left, the credit was fully used for that day. If it's less, that's how much they used.
  4. You must manually add up the daily usage to find the remaining balance.
If The Guest Has Unused Credits:
  1. Comp the guest for the unused amount in the billing window. You may need to split charges to get the exact amount.
  2. Move that amount to a comp window (106, 107).
  3. Use the appropriate authorizer (from stay details), set the profile to Last Name = salesandservices | First Name = fdk.
  4. Refund the guest and send an updated folio.

Fixed Charge

  1. This is for items requested by the guest that have a cost, like a rollaway bed or extra fridge.
  2. Select the item (e.g., rollaway bed) and add the charge (e.g., $50).
  3. Add notes confirming you informed the guest of the daily price.
